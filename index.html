Introduction

I am a chemical engineer by training, with a passion for advancing the fundamental understanding of materials at the atomic scale. My fascination with atoms began in high school and guided my academic journey. I graduated with an undergraduate degree in Chemical Engineering from Jadavpur University, Kolkata, India — home to the oldest chemical engineering department in Asia — where I also gained valuable experience in experimental research. After clearing the GATE examination, I pursued a master’s degree at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bengaluru.As fate would have it, during my time at IISc, I was reintroduced to the world of atoms and computer simulations. This dormant interest was reignited, drawing me back to the fascinating world of atomic-scale science. Under the guidance of Dr. Ananth Govind Rajan in the Department of Chemical Engineering at IISc, I worked on predicting nanopore shapes in 2D materials using ab initio methods. Since then, I have actively pursued research in electronic structure theory-based methods and their applications to solve scientific problems.Currently, I am a doctoral candidate in the School of Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering at the Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta, USA. I am keenly interested in atomistic simulations and in modeling and simulation of materials. I collaborate with two research groups: the Material Physics & Mechanics Group led by Dr. Phanish Suryanarayana, and the Medford Group led by Dr. Andrew J. Medford, who both serve as my PhD advisors. As part of this work, I contribute to the development of SPARC, an open-source density functional theory code led by Dr. Suryanarayana, which allows me to connect theory, algorithms, and practical applications.My work bridges practical applications with a rigorous dive into the foundations of electronic structure methods, helping to push the boundaries of computational materials science. Beyond research, I have always loved mathematics and enjoy solving challenging problems. My hobbies include reading and painting; although I am not an avid reader, I love getting lost in storybooks. I began painting more regularly during my time at IISc, which has become a wonderful way to spend weekends and holidays. I also love to travel, explore new places, and experience diverse cultures.
